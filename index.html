THE FULL STORY

The Journey of David Amoateng

Six chapters tracing the road from Wassa Mampong to Moscow.

I

FIRST STAR ACADEMY · WASSA MAMPONG

The Boy from Wassa Mampong

They called him Dave. The last born, the only boy on his mother's side, the fourth son of his father — a child who somehow carried the quiet weight of everyone's hopes without ever buckling beneath them.

He was born to Op. Kojo Amoateng and Adwoa Ntiakowaa, two farmers whose lives had been given to the soil of Wassa Mampong. From his earliest years, Dave knew the rhythm of the land: the early mornings, the weight of a hoe, and the patience of waiting for something planted to grow.

“Keep pushing him, even if it takes your last penny and your last breath.” — His teachers, First Star Academy

Young Dave dreamt of the stars. He wanted to be an astronomer, to work with NASA, and to trace constellations through a telescope. He did not know then that his own life would become the constellation worth tracing.

II

SIMMS SENIOR HIGH SCHOOL · KUMASI · 2013–2016

The City That Almost Swallowed Him

When the BECE results came in 2013, Dave had passed. But passing meant leaving Wassa Mampong, leaving the familiar scent of home, and entering the larger world of Kumasi.

First year was brutal. In junior high, Dave had always floated in the top three. At SIMMS, surrounded by sharp minds and louder voices, he sat in the 31st position out of 55 students.

31st 9th 7th 5th 2nd

Slowly, quietly, the numbers began to shift until one golden term he became the second-best General Arts student in his class. He had not only survived the city; he had learned its language.

III

UNIVERSITY OF GHANA, LEGON · ACCRA · 2017–2021

The Vandal

The University of Ghana, Legon, was never part of the plan. Dave had intended to apply to KNUST, but a nudge from his niece changed the direction of his life. The admission letter arrived. Then another came from KNUST. But when the best option appears before you, you take it.

At Legon, he studied Geography & Resource Development alongside Political Science. He became a proud member of Commonwealth Hall — the Vandals — and found a brotherhood that gave him confidence, networks, and voice.

He met Silas, founder of the IANJAP poetry community, who helped him speak in rooms, hold his ground in discussions, and command attention with substance. Then COVID came in 2020 and scattered everything, but Dave stayed the course.

In 2021, David Amoateng graduated from the University of Ghana, Legon, with First Class Honours.

IV

NATIONAL SERVICE · PRESEC LEGON · 2021–2022

The Long Wait

After graduation, Dave served as a teacher at Presbyterian Boys' Secondary School — PRESEC Legon. It was humbling and grounding to stand before students and hold young futures in his hands the same way his own teachers once held his.

Then service ended, and the silence came. Eight months of sitting at home, sending applications, watching the world move while he stood still.

He eventually became a Risk and Compliance Officer at the head office of Pacific Savings and Loans PLC. But deep inside, Dave knew this was not the final chapter.

V

ACCRA → MOSCOW · NOVEMBER 2023 – SEPTEMBER 2024

Zeroman's Call

The call came from an unlikely place — from the past, from a junior high school classmate, academic rival, brother in Christ, and the man who had made it almost impossible for Dave to ever claim first position in class. They called him Zeroman.

“Why are you still in Ghana after graduating with first class?” — Zeroman, November 2023

It was not criticism. It was a door opening. Together, they worked through applications, requirements, and deadlines. In September 2024, David Amoateng boarded a plane for Moscow.

Russia arrived like a wall: the cold, the language, the distance, the new culture. But at the airport, Zeroman was waiting. He helped with dormitory registration and the essential steps that turned arrival into belonging.

VI

HSE UNIVERSITY · MOSCOW, RUSSIA

HSE Is Not for the Weak

The National Research University — Higher School of Economics — demanded everything. Dave enrolled in the Master's programme in Population and Development, within the field of Public Administration, and the programme made him earn every credit.

There were moments when leaving seemed easier than staying. The language barrier isolated him. Academic pressure mounted. Russian winter tested him. Homesickness followed him. But the voice of Wassa Mampong was not easily silenced.
